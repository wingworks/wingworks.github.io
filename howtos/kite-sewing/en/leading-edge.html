Cut segments

Leading Edge may burst or warp

For a precise shape and high strength, the alignment of the Dacron must be considered:
  • Cut all segments out of the canvas exactly in the longitudinal direction (chain).
  • Glue segments together for right and left sides and cut them together.
  • The seam course and markings may deviate a maximum of 1mm from the specifications.

Create cutting template for the MDF leading edge segments

  1. Stick double-sided tape along the side cut lines of the segments from below onto the cutting pattern of the leading edge segments [ LE-1 ].
  2. Stick the pattern on a 3mm thick MDF board.
  3. Drill a 3mm diameter hole into each corner.
  4. Drill a 3mm diameter hole into each tie mark.
  5. Saw out the cutting lines with a handy jigsaw and a narrow saw blade (max 1mm). At the beginning and end, each cut about 1cm in the direction of the other end.
  6. Place cutting template exactly in the direction of the chain on the Dacron and secure against slipping with weights.
  7. Cut segments [ LE-1 ] with a cutter. [caption id = "attachment_3507" align = "alignnone" width = "300"] Cutting template made of MDF [/ caption]
  8. Label segments so that the inside (bladder) + outside (patches) can be distinguished later.
  9. Lay the segments on the cutting template from above and mark the marking lines for the connection with the sail.
  10. Mark the position of the attachment points [ BAP ] on the segments.

shutter

  1. Place the last segment on the table with the inside up.
  2. Cut out the two parts of the lock [ FLAP-LE-1 ] and the reinforcement [ RF-LE-2 ] as planned.
  3. Sew Velcro tape [ VEL-1 ] (Velcro) on the inside of the segment | S-60-4 |.
  4. Sew Velcro tape [ VEL-2 ] (hook) to the bottom of [ FLAP-LE-1 ] | S-60-4 |.
  5. [ FLAP-LE-1 ] with seam | ZZZ-60-4-8 | Sew on the segment from the inside. [ VEL-2 ] points down.
  6. Both sides of [ FLAP-LE-1 with seam | S-60-4 | Sew.
  7. Turn the segment over and place it on the table with the outside facing up.
  8. Stick on [ RF-LE-2 ] and sew on | ZZZ-60-4-8 | fasten. Attention: Do not sew on the closure [ FLAP-LE-1 ]!
  9. Flip [ FLAP-LE-1 ].
  10. Fold seam allowance at the end of the segment together with [ RF-LE-2 ] and seam | ZZZ-60-4-8 | fasten.

zipper

  1. Glue reinforcements [ RF-LE-3 ] to the insides of both segments [ LE-1 ] and laterally with seam | ZZZ-60-4-8 | fasten.
  2. Make zipper insert from the side reinforcements [ RF-LE-5 ], the zipper [ PRT-LE-3 ], the front and rear reinforcements and the zipper cover [ PRT-LE-1 ]. For all seams seam | S-60-4 | use.
  3. Attach I-cut for zipper in left [ LE-1 ] segment, fold edges and glue.
  4. Stick zipper insert on the inside.
  5. Sew on zipper insert all around.
  6. Sew an additional seam to the left of the zipper.
  7. Bladder protection cover [ PRT-LE-1 ] (shown here in transparent) from the inside to the right of the zipper.
  8. Sew on the right side of the zipper from the outside.

valves

  1. Mark the position of the valves on the reinforcement [ RL-LE-3 ].
  2. Affix reinforcement to [ RF-LE-4 ] and sew.
  3. Depending on the valve used, fit the appropriate cutouts.
  4. [ RF-LE-4 ] all around with seam | S-60-4 | fasten.
  5. Depending on the valve used, sew on the fuses.
  6. Optional: Sew on straps for camera bracket.

Attachment points for the balance leashes

Tip: In order to customize the flight behavior additional attachment points should be sewn.
  1. Fold webbing [ BAP ] and at a distance M = 8mm from the bend with seam | S-60-2 | Sew.
  2. Stick double-sided adhesive tape from the outside onto [ BAP ].
  3. Pull apart [ BAP ] and glue onto the segment from the inside.
  4. Glue reinforcement [ RF-LE-1 ] from the inside over [ BAP ].
  5. [ BAP ] and [ RF-LE-1 ] from outside with seam | ZZZ-60-5-10 | fasten.
  6. Repeat steps for all attachment points

Sew segments together

Twisted Leading Edge

If the segment seams are not exactly on top of each other (tolerance + -0.2mm) then the leading edge will go wrong and twist. [gallery ids = "1721,1716"]
  1. Stick double-sided adhesive tape (6mm wide) along the cut line on the outside of the segment.
  2. Glue the second segment [ LE-2 ] with the outside onto [ LE-1 ] so that the start, end, cut edges and sail lines are exactly on top of each other.
  3. With seam | S-30-4 | Sew the segment seam at a distance of 7mm from the cutting edge. It is essential to use a lateral stop so that the seam runs exactly parallel to the cut and the reference distances are exactly maintained.
  4. Thread ends merge.
  5. Stick [ RF-LE-9 ] with 1-2mm protrusion along the cutting line to the inner segment, cut laterally depending on the curve and press firmly.
  6. Unfold the segments and fold the stitched end outwards in the direction of the tip.
  7. On both sides of the segment seam from outside a seam | ZZZ-60-4-8 | sew.
  8. Repeat for all segments until the entire leading edge is sewn together.

Reinforcements outside (scuff protection)

  1. Cut reinforcements [ RF-LE-10 ].
  2. The edges of [ RF-LE-10 ] and [ RF-LE-11 ] with lighter or soldering iron merge so that they do not fray.
  3. Stick double-sided tape to the back of [ RF-LE-10 ] and [ RF-LE-11 ].
  4. Glue [ RF-LE-10 ] smoothly over the segment seam of the leading edge. Start is 1cm above the sail seam (M1).
  5. [ RF-LE-10 ] side and bottom with seam | ZZZ-60-4-8 | sew. The upper seam is sewn later together with the sail seam.
  6. Glue [ RF-LE-11 ] in the center of the kit smoothly over the segment seam of the leading edge. Start is 3cm before the seal.
  7. [ RF-LE-11 ] side and bottom with seam | ZZZ-60-4-8 | sew. The seam ends 1cm in front of the sail marking line.
