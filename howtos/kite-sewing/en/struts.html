Part 5 - Connecting Leading Edge and Sails

Cut out segments

  1. Lay the pattern for the first strut segment across the canvas so that the strut runs exactly in the direction of the weft. Secure pattern with weights to prevent slipping.
  2. Transfer cutting lines of the segment connections on the sides with copy wheel onto the canvas.
  3. Mark the straight cut lines at the beginning and end with a copy wheel.
  4. Cut out with a rotary cutter segment. Use a ruler for the straight cut lines.
  5. Repeat the steps for all other strut segments.
  6. Cut out the first segment [ ST-1 ] again and glue both segments [ ST-1 ] together with double-sided adhesive tape along the cut edges.
  7. Glue reinforcement for valve [ RF-ST-1 ] from inside to [ ST-1 ] and all around with seam | S-60-4 | fasten.
  1. On the foremost segment inside and outside 9mm double-sided adhesive stick along the cutting line.
  2. Affix Reinforcement [ RF-ST-2 ] onto the transition from [ ST-1 ] to Leading Edge flush with the cut edge and with Seam | S-60-4 | and seam | ZZ-60-4-8 | Sew.

shutter

  1. Cut out the cap [ FLAP-ST-1 ] and patch [ RF-ST-5 ].
  2. Place the last segment [ ST-6 ] on the table, face down.
  3. Glue Velcro tape [ VEL-1 ] (Velcro) to the inside of the segment and sew with seam | S-60-4 | sew all around.
  4. Sew on Velcro [ VEL-2 ] on the underside of [ FLAP-ST-1 ] | S-60-4 | ,
  5. [ FLAP-ST-1 ] with seam | ZZ-60-4-8 | Sew on the segment from the inside. [ VEL-2 ] points down.
  6. Fold [ FLAP-ST-1 ] in the middle.
  7. [ FLAP-ST-1 ] with seam | S-60-4 | close. Attention: do not sew the segment!
  8. Turn the segment over and place it face down on the table.
  9. Glue [ RF-ST-5 ] onto [ ST-6 ] and sew with | ZZ-60-4-8 | Sew on the front and sides. Attention: Do not sew on the closure [ FLAP-ST-1 ]!
  10. Flip [ FLAP-ST-1 ].
  11. Fold seam allowance at the end of the segment together with [ RF-ST-5 ] and seam | ZZ-60-4-8 | fasten.

Sew segments together

Prevent warped strut: If the segment seams are not exactly aligned (tolerance + -0.25mm) then the strut goes awry and the kite does not fly straight ahead. [gallery columns = "5" ids = "1716"]
  1. Stick double-sided tape along the cutting line on the outside of the segment.
  2. Glue the second segment with the outside onto the first segment, so that the cut edges, sail lines and alignment points are exactly on top of each other.
  3. With seam | S-30-4 | sew the segment seam at a distance of 7mm to the cutting edge.
  4. Thread ends merge.
  5. Unfold the segments and fold the sewn end backwards.
  6. On the rear side of the segment seam from the outside seam | ZZZ-60-4-8 | sew.
  7. Repeat for all segments until the entire strut is sewn together.
  8. Depending on the valve used, attach a suitable opening for the valve and all around with seam | S-60-4 | fasten.

Transition to the leading edge

  1. Adhere 9mm wide double-sided adhesive tape along the cutting edge on both sides of the foremost segment.
  2. Glue elastic band [ RF-ST-2 ] to the outside of the segment so that the center of the band lies exactly on the cutting line.
  3. Fold the tape around the cutting edge and glue it together
  4. With seam | ZZZ-60-4-8 | sew the tape to the segment.
  5. Reinforcement [ RF-ST-2 ] with seam | ZZZ-60-4-8 | sew on the strut.

Close tightly

  1. Stick double-sided tape along the entire cutting edge on the inside of the strut.
  2. Stick together so that alignment points, cut edges and segment seams are exactly on top of each other.
  3. At a distance of 13mm -A- to the cutting edge the strut with seam | S-40-4 | close.
  4. Seam allowance along the straight seam -A- fold and with a second seam with seam | S-40-4 | Sew in at a distance of 3mm -B- to the first seam.

Sew to the Leading Edge

Damage to the leading edge possible

The following steps are almost impossible to do the first time. It takes some practice and patience. Therefore, they should first be practiced on a pattern.
  1. Transfer external marking line for the strut from the blueprint to the leading edge. You can use a paper template for this purpose.
  2. Stick a ring of 6mm wide double-sided adhesive tape inside the marking line.
  3. Strut with the elastic band at the top and bottom flush with the adhesive tape on the Leading Edge and stick up and down to about 1cm in length with individual stitches so that the strut no longer slip ..
  4. Stick the elastic band all around flush with the adhesive tape on the leading edge.
  5. Strut run around with seam | S-40-4 | Sew to the Leading Edge.
