Sew sails on Leading Edge

  1. Glue 9mm wide double-sided tape along the sail line onto the leading edge.
  2. Glue sails from the center along the sail line onto the leading edge. Each segment seam must lie exactly in the middle of the sail overlap.
  3. Sails and Leading Edge with seam | ZZZ-60-5-10 | Sew.
  4. Double stitch the last segment with the attachment points of the control lines.

Sew on the sail

  1. Transfer markings for the segment seams from the blueprint to the sail.
  2. Stick double-sided adhesive tape to the strut.
  3. Align with the sail so that the markings match the segment seams.
  4. Bonding strut and sail.
  5. Strut and sail with seam | ZZZ-60-4-8 | fasten.

Sew patches on strut

  1. Trim Reinforcement [ RF-ST-6 ] for the leech.
  2. Glue reinforcement on the sail at the end of the strut and with seam | ZZZ-60-4-8 | fasten.
  3. Glue reinforcement [ RF-ST-11 ] straight on the sail and with seam | ZZZ-60-4-8 | fasten.

Close Leading Edge

Prevent strength loss

If the seams are not absolutely sewn straight then they can burst at high pressure.

Prevent warped Leading Edge

If marker holes and segment seams are not exactly aligned, the leading edge will go awry and the kite will not fly straight ahead. [gallery ids = "1721,1716"]

Avoid damage to the kite

The closing seam on the kite is exposed to very high loads in flight and in a crash. Therefore, it must be sewn very carefully. Before you do the seam on the leading edge, you should practice on several pieces of remnants and make sure that your sewing machine is powerful enough to sew 10 layers of polyester cloth with 5 layers of adhesive tape. If that is not possible with your sewing machine you can have the seam made by me.
