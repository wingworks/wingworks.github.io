Important information for using the kite blueprint

Abbreviations are always in square brackets. Example: [ RF-LE-10 ]. Which part it concerns exactly, from which material it manufactured and how often it is needed is in the parts list.

seam names

Seam details are structured according to the following scheme: | Stitch Pattern Thickness Stitch Length (SL) Stitch Width (SW) |.

stitch pattern

ZZZ 3-fold zigzag stitch (also sailmaker stitch)
ZZ Normal zigzag stitch
S Straight stitch

denier

Information about the strength or weight of the yarn to be used. Information in the Nm system.

Stitch width (SW)

Information about the width of the seam across the seam direction. At ZZ and ZZZ, it is the distance between the outermost suture holes. Figures in mm. With straight stitch S, the stitch width is always 0, therefore this information is omitted.

Stitch length (SL)

Information about the distance of the stitch holes in the seam direction. For straight stitch S, it is the distance between the individual holes, for ZZ and ZZZ the distance between the outermost seam holes. In mm.

Examples

| ZZZ-60-6-8 | : Triple Zigzag with 60s Yarn, Stitch Width 6mm and Stitch Length 8mm | S-30-4 | : Straight stitch with 30s thread and stitch length 4mm

needles

The needle size depends on the sewn material, so it can not always be given a flat rate. Sampling is useful to choose the right needle. An orientation gives the following table:
twine needle material
80 80 Spinnaker + spinnaker
80 90 Spinnaker + Dacron
60 100 Spinnaker + Dacron multilayer
60 110 Dacron + Dacron
40 120 Dacron multilayer
